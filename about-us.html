    What is the TFL Fellowship?

    The Fellowship is a Leadership Development Program. Teacher Fellows gain professional training and support while they expand opportunities for students in the highest-need schools. Fellows attend a teacher training institute, lead a classroom for two years with coaching and support from TFL staff, and develop the leadership and professional skills for the next steps in their careers in the classroom and beyond. As Alumni of the program, past fellows benefit from support ranging from opportunities to apply and secure funding for graduate school to placement in roles in the private and public sector.

    Charles Dean Wilson

    Founder and CEO,Charles Dean Wilson: A consummated professional of six years of military experience and ten years of humanitarian voluntary service; Charles Dean Wilson embarked on a career of patriotism , in the US Navy and was deployed to Guantanamo Bay, Cuba while graduating community college at Tarrant County College.. After years in the military and upon discharge, the CEO sought a degree at West Chester University, obtaining a degree in Public Health and upon graduation; organized a community program for youths at risk; all year around, where interpersonal skills and community volunteering was the focus. That program grew to close to a hundred youths who had intramural athletic leagues during the summer and continues to operate in the southwest community area of Philadelphia. During a visit to his parents country of origin, the Ebola pandemic struck and again, the CEO found himself volunteered his service in a public health effort; joining groups of community leaders to stress safety protocols , in an effort to reach the poor and vulnerable with disinfected measures.
